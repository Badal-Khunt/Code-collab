# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

CollabCode is an open and collaborative project. We aim to create a respectful, inclusive, and productive environment where all contributors feel valued and safe.

This Code of Conduct outlines expected behavior when participating in this project and is intended to guide positive and effective collaboration.

---

## Why We Contribute

Contributors may join this project for many reasons, including:
- Improving technical and collaboration skills
- Fixing bugs or improving features they use
- Helping others learn and contribute more effectively
- Demonstrating skills for academic or professional growth

All of these motivations are valid. Having clear intentions helps keep contributions meaningful and productive.

---

## Expected Behaviour

All contributors are expected to:

- Be polite, respectful, and kind in all interactions
- Use clear, constructive, and professional language
- Thank and acknowledge others for their contributions
- Offer feedback in a supportive and helpful manner
- Stay on topic and keep discussions relevant
- Be patient and considerate of others’ time
- Read project documentation before asking questions
- Ask questions in the appropriate channels
- Respect project decisions, even when disagreeing

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech of any kind
- Offensive, abusive, or inflammatory language
- Personal attacks or assumptions about others’ background, identity, or experience
- Repeatedly disrupting discussions or going off-topic
- Demanding immediate responses or decisions
- Creating unnecessary noise through vague or unproductive issues

---

## Making Effective Contributions

To keep the project productive, contributors should:

### Do:
- Discuss one topic per issue
- Submit one clear fix per pull request
- Ensure contributions are meaningful and aligned with project goals
- Help others by answering questions where appropriate

### Avoid:
- Making cosmetic or stylistic changes without purpose
- Bundling multiple unrelated fixes into a single pull request
- Opening issues without attempting to understand the problem first

---

## Communication and Decision Making

This project encourages open discussion and collaboration. However, some decisions may be made by the project lead or maintainers to ensure progress and consistency.

All contributors are expected to respect final decisions, even if they differ from their own opinions.

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that makes you uncomfortable, please report it to the project lead. All reports will be reviewed fairly and handled respectfully.

---

## Enforcement

Failure to follow this Code of Conduct may result in:
- A warning
- Temporary restriction from participation
- Removal from the project in serious cases

These measures exist to protect contributors and maintain a healthy project environment.

---

## Commitment

By participating in this project, you agree to follow this Code of Conduct and help maintain a positive and collaborative community.
